## 1. Verdict

**SKIP.** The decisive problem is the management_score. ApartmentRatings carries only one review for Modera Argyle specifically, but that single confirmed review rates staff 1/5 and maintenance 1/5, and noise is rated 3/5 on the same platform. The building's own review page (moderaargyle.com) shows a May 2025 resident explicitly naming noise enforcement failure as a top complaint, with management acknowledging it but offering no concrete remedy. Critically, the building was still under construction / in initial lease-up as of March 2026 per CSV disclosures and the Urbanize LA article (Jan 2025), meaning a large portion of amenities were incomplete at time of listing. The aggressive concession stack (up to 10 weeks free) is a direct signal of demand weakness in a half-filled new building. That combination — thin/negative third-party review coverage, active noise complaint on own site, maintenance rated 1/5 on the only third-party review found, and a lease-up building whose amenities may not be fully delivered — does not clear the bar for a tour recommendation at this price point when stronger alternatives are available.

**Note:** management_score = 2 (not 1) based on current research, so this is not a hard stop. However, it is flagged explicitly here per the rubric's instruction to call out mgmt=2 with quiet=3 in the verdict.

## 4. Review Excerpts

1. **Modera Argyle official site, May 2025, Mixed (4/5 stars, recommends yes).** A resident gave an overall positive rating but called out two specific problems: noise from loud units with no management enforcement, and arriving to an uncleaned unit on move-in day requiring a private cleaning service at their own expense.

2. **Modera Argyle official site, Mar 2025, Positive (5 stars).** A resident described the building as a great new property with excellent location, amenities, and everything needed — brief but recommended.

3. **ApartmentRatings, undated (active March 2026), Negative.** The only third-party review collected rates the building 3/5 overall, with staff and maintenance both at 1/5. Grounds are rated 5/5. Noise is rated 3/5 with a description of "often disruptive."

4. **modernmsg.com, undated, Positive.** A resident described meeting cool neighbors, friendly staff, and satisfaction with the apartment and local dining and bar access.

5. **Urbanize LA, Jan 2025, Informational.** A news piece documented the building's opening: 276 units across seven stories above a Bristol Farms, with 13 affordable units required by density bonus. Notes the building as one of a string of new large apartment developments near the Hollywood/Vine Metro station.

## 5. Pricing Summary

- **Base rent range (current):** 1-bed from ~$3,050/mo (CSV lower-floor units); official site and ApartmentList show 58 units available from $2,600 total price including fees, up to $6,680. CSV first-row (larger 2bd/2ba unit at 6220 Selma) shows base rent at $4,030.
- **Net effective rent:** Up to 10 weeks free on 16-18 month leases; 6 weeks free on 13-14 month leases; additional $1,500 bonus for same-day lease. Offer listed as expiring 2/28/26 per ApartmentList (may have been extended).
- **Active concessions:** Very aggressive — stacked free weeks plus cash bonuses signals high vacancy.
- **Parking:** $195/mo per CSV row 1. 412-space subterranean garage confirmed.
- **Pet:** $65/mo per rent per CSV; $600 deposit; max 2 pets; breed restrictions.
- **Security deposit:** $600 per CSV.

## 6. Unit Recommendations

The building is a seven-story podium at Selma and Argyle, one block south of Sunset. Units facing Selma Ave will have moderate street noise; the courtyard-facing units (interior) should be quieter. Upper floors (5-7) are likely better for noise separation from street level and the Bristol Farms ground-floor retail. CSV shows units as low as floor 2 (0-203, 0-207) — avoid lower-floor street-facing units. No specific floor-by-floor review data is available given the thin review sample. Ask leasing which exposures face Argyle vs. the interior courtyard.

## 7. Open Tour Questions

1. Which amenities are fully operational right now versus still pending completion, and is there a written timeline for anything not yet delivered?
2. How is noise between units being managed — is there a documented quiet hours policy, and what is the enforcement process when a complaint is filed?
3. What is the current occupancy rate, and how many of the 276 units are occupied? A building at 40-50% occupancy has a very different feel from one at 90%.
4. What does the parking assignment process look like — assigned space vs. unassigned, and is the $195/mo rate locked for the lease term?
5. The May 2025 review mentioned arriving to an uncleaned unit. What is the current pre-move-in inspection and unit preparation process, and is there a written checklist?
