## 1. Verdict

**SKIP.** Sunset Vine Tower has a genuine split personality in its review record: Google shows 4.2/5 across 66 reviews with consistently warm recent mentions of named staff (Tony in maintenance, Lizz and Oliver in leasing, Kevin and Oscar at valet/front desk), but ApartmentRatings carries two detailed negative reviews that document structural operational failures — one maintenance person for an entire high-rise, neighbor smoke and noise going unaddressed through 6-7am, package theft with management disclaiming responsibility, and a building manager who sat in the same room as complaints and said nothing for six months. The building is also at 1480 Vine, directly at the Sunset and Vine intersection, which is one of the noisiest street corners in Hollywood. That location means any street-facing unit will carry ambient noise regardless of management quality. The combination of a confirmed noise-enforcement gap, a confirmed location noise exposure, confirmed single-tech maintenance bandwidth, and three essential amenities missing from CSV unit rows (dishwasher, central A/C, package room) pushes this below the tour threshold when stronger options are available.

## 4. Review Excerpts

1. **ApartmentRatings, undated (long-term resident, 2 years), Negative.** A two-year resident documented: neighbors smoking indoors triggering fire alarms at 3-4am regularly; parties audible through 6-7am with no enforcement response; one maintenance tech for the whole building causing 2-3 day delays; packages stolen with management disclaiming liability; security absent from post; building manager physically present but nonverbal through multiple in-person complaints over six months.

2. **ApartmentRatings, second review, Negative.** A resident described management as having no authority to spend money or act independently, so any problem requiring outside-hours resolution got no response. Mentioned possible carbon monoxide concerns from A/C tampering that were never addressed despite follow-up.

3. **Google (via Birdeye), Jan 2026, Positive.** A resident praised the building's vibe and aesthetics and called out Tony (maintenance), the 24/7 front desk, and valet staff Kevin and Oscar by name as consistently helpful.

4. **Google (via Birdeye), 4 months ago (~late 2025), Positive.** A new resident praised Oliver for a professional leasing experience, described the community as clean and safe-feeling.

5. **Yelp (Alliance era, now closed listing), undated, Positive.** An early resident praised the controlled-access elevators, 5-star concierge staff, and 15th-floor views, describing the building as unmatched among Hollywood complexes they had toured.

## 6. Unit Recommendations

Building is 20 stories at the Sunset/Vine intersection — one of the loudest corners in Hollywood. Units on floors 15-20 facing away from Sunset Blvd and Vine St (i.e., north or east-facing toward quieter residential streets) will have the best noise profile. Lower floors facing the intersection are not viable for a noise-sensitive renter. CSV shows "Loft A" inventory at units 0804 and 1004 — floor 8 and 10, which are mid-rise on what is likely a loft-style layout. Floor 10 at minimum, floor 15+ preferred. Ask specifically which direction the unit faces before committing to a tour.

## 7. Open Tour Questions

1. How many maintenance technicians are currently on staff, and what is the stated SLA for urgent vs. routine repair requests?
2. What is the package security situation — is there a staffed desk, a locker system, or an Amazon Hub? The ApartmentRatings review describes package theft with management disclaiming responsibility.
3. Is smoking (tobacco or cannabis) permitted in any units, and what is the enforcement process when a violation is reported?
4. What is the all-in monthly cost — base rent plus parking, trash/utility billing, and any mandatory fees?
5. Who is the current building manager (not the leasing agent), how long have they been in the role, and how is after-hours escalation handled when leasing staff are unavailable?
