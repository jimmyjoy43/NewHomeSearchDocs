## 1. Verdict

**SKIP.** ZEN Hollywood has two confirmed structural problems that cannot be fixed by management changes. First, a detailed Google review (Feb 2025, 1-star) describes the walls and floors as paper-thin — whole apartment shakes when upstairs neighbors walk, confirmed by management themselves as a known construction defect they cannot remedy. Second, the building has a confirmed balcony-climbing intrusion pattern: a March 2024 break-in on the 3rd floor with documented video evidence, management's belated email notification four days later, and a separate reviewer mentioning "break-ins and homeless people jumping in over almost every week." This is a physical security vulnerability from the building's design (accessible balconies on lower floors) that management cannot structurally address. The Google rating is 3.2/5 with a bimodal distribution: 12 five-stars and 10 one-stars, with zero reviews in the 2-4 range. That pattern is a classic signal of a building where some residents love the amenities and new management while others have had genuinely unsafe experiences. The amenity stack is genuinely strong and the post-Bozzuto management reviews from late 2024 are encouraging, but the construction quality issues and proven physical security gap make this not viable for a noise-sensitive renter prioritizing safety and quiet.

## 4. Review Excerpts

1. **Google (via Wanderlog), Jun 2024, 1-star.** A resident who moved in Feb 2024 and moved out May 2024 documented a confirmed March 2024 break-in: an intruder climbed the building and entered at least two apartments on the 2nd and 3rd floors. Management issued no welfare check and notified residents four days later via email. Resident left due to these security failures.

2. **Google (via Wanderlog), Feb 2025, 1-star.** A reviewer described the building as having paper-thin walls and floors — the entire apartment shakes when upstairs neighbors take steps. Management confirmed this is a known construction defect they cannot fix. Also mentioned ongoing balcony intrusions by homeless individuals as a near-weekly occurrence.

3. **Google (via Wanderlog), Jul 2024, 5-star.** A former resident who moved out due to a career change praised the building's amenities (gym, pool, game room, conference rooms) and described the new post-Bozzuto management as exceptionally responsive, prompt, and professional.

4. **Google (via Wanderlog), Dec 2024, 5-star.** A resident praised the management change from Bozzuto and specifically named new manager Janeth as helpful, attentive, and someone who made them finally feel safe and comfortable in the building.

5. **RentCafe, May 2025, Positive.** A new resident described a pleasant experience: friendly staff and neighbors, good views, calming atmosphere, and specifically noted that outside noise from the street does not penetrate the apartment much — a contrast to the interior floor-ceiling noise problem documented in other reviews.

## 6. Unit Recommendations

Avoid floors 2-3 entirely given the confirmed balcony-climbing intrusion pattern — these are physically accessible from the exterior. Units on floors 4+ are meaningfully safer from that specific vector. The Feb 2025 reviewer describes apartments shaking from upstairs neighbors; top-floor units eliminate the neighbor-above problem but may not resolve lateral sound transmission. The building is on Las Palmas Ave between Hollywood Blvd and Franklin — a relatively quiet residential side street compared to Vine or Santa Monica Blvd. Units facing Las Palmas should have low street noise. Avoid the Highland Ave side per the reviewer who noted balconies collecting dirt and smoke from that exposure.
