## 1. Verdict

**SKIP.** The building has a good amenity stack and AvalonBay is an institutional operator with accountability infrastructure, but two confirmed structural problems push this below the tour threshold for a noise-sensitive renter. First, the building has a documented ceiling/floor sound transmission defect: a Yelp forum response with 11 helpful votes confirms you can hear upstairs neighbors simply walking, not even stomping. This is a construction-quality issue, not an enforcement issue, meaning it cannot be fixed by management. Second, the ApartmentRatings review — while from the COVID era — confirms the same noise problem independently and adds that management was unresponsive and refused to negotiate during hardship. Yelp shows 2.7 stars across 113 reviews (the headline number; full text is paywalled), which is a significant negative signal for a large institutional building. The location on Santa Monica Blvd also adds street noise. Combined with the CSV already flagging five missing essential amenities on most unit rows (in-unit laundry, dishwasher, central A/C, package room, controlled access), this building does not warrant a tour over stronger alternatives in the queue.

## 4. Review Excerpts

1. **Yelp forum, ~2020, Negative (11 helpful votes).** A resident responded to a direct question about sound insulation and confirmed that the ceiling — not the walls — transmits sound clearly enough to hear an upstairs neighbor simply walking. The reviewer described waking at 6am daily to the sound of a neighbor getting ready for work, calling it "absolutely terrible."

2. **ApartmentRatings, COVID era, Negative.** A resident described an extended dispute with management during COVID: they made consistent payments for 1.5 years despite financial hardship, were denied a co-tenant release, attempted to renegotiate the lease, and were ultimately given no option but to leave. Fire alarm testing ran for over a year with no resolution. Management described as inflexible and robotic.

3. **veryapt.com, Oct 2023, Positive.** A brief review praised amenities (pool, rooftop, gym, study lounge), described the location as convenient to nearby schools, and highlighted the area's dining and coffee options.

4. **ApartmentList (aggregated), March 2026, Neutral.** Platform notes the building offers 24 available units starting at $2,165/mo and is collecting verified resident reviews — meaning no verified reviews are yet published on that platform.

5. **Yelp headline, March 2026, Negative signal.** The Yelp listing shows 2.7 stars across 113 reviews. Full review text is behind robots.txt and could not be retrieved, but a 2.7/5 on 113 reviews for a large institutional building is a significant negative signal worth treating as confirmed negative sentiment even without specific excerpts.

## 6. Unit Recommendations

Avoid any unit directly below another unit — the confirmed ceiling sound transmission defect means top-floor units are the only reliable mitigation. Building is 6 stories; request floor 6 if touring. Units facing Santa Monica Blvd will have additional street noise — request interior or side-street exposures. CSV unit rows are all labeled building 003, floors 32-34 (unit numbers like 003-3414, 003-3418, 003-3222), suggesting a specific building or tower within the complex. The building has 695 units across multiple structures per the site map, so clarify which structure and exposure you're being shown.

## 7. Open Tour Questions

1. What floor is the unit on, and are any top-floor units available? The building has a confirmed ceiling sound transmission issue — only the top floor eliminates the upstairs neighbor variable.
2. Are in-unit washer/dryer, dishwasher, and central A/C standard in the unit being shown, or are those building-level amenities only accessible in common areas?
3. What is the all-in monthly cost including parking, trash/utility fees, and any mandatory amenity fees beyond base rent?
4. How does management handle noise complaints between units — is there a documented process and response timeline?
5. What is the breakdown of short-term (Furnished+) vs. standard long-term leases in the building, and are both types housed in the same building/tower?
