## 1. Verdict

**SKIP.** Domain WeHo has two confirmed, independent structural noise complaints — one on ApartmentRatings and one on Yelp — both describing the same problem: thin walls and poor inter-unit sound insulation that causes neighbor noise at all hours. The Yelp reviewer is explicit that this is a building construction quality issue ("the building is very cheaply made"), not a neighbor behavior problem. That makes it unfixable by management. The building also has a documented track record of units handed over in unusable condition (multiple broken sockets, non-functional appliances), and the overall ApartmentRatings sentiment is negative ("complete nightmare," "worst place I've ever lived"). The West Hollywood location and amenity stack are genuinely good, but a building with structural noise defects cannot pass the quiet_score threshold regardless of what else it offers.

## 4. Review Excerpts

1. **ApartmentRatings, undated, Negative.** A resident described the unit as unlivable at move-in: approximately 30% of electrical sockets non-functional, stove broken for 7 days, washer/dryer broken for 13 days. Required persistent follow-up to get basic repairs done. Described the experience as "a complete nightmare" and directed prospective tenants to Yelp.

2. **Yelp (Lincoln-era listing), ~2019, Negative.** A current resident at time of writing described the building as "very cheaply made" with very poor sound insulation — able to hear neighbors moving around at all times of night. Also described elevators as slow and consistently fouled with dog urine.

3. **ApartmentRatings, undated, Positive.** A resident praised the community for its proximity to shops, the pool and jacuzzi with a view, the outdoor BBQ and pizza oven setup, friendly neighbors, and good office staff. Named a specific staff member (Lora) positively.

4. **Yelp (Alliance-era listing, now closed), various dates.** The older listing accumulated 41 reviews under the prior management company. The headline Yelp rating across both listings points to long-standing building reputation issues, not just management-transition problems.

5. **domainweho.com (official), current.** Marketing copy explicitly frames the building around social/nightlife culture: rooftop retreat, pool, "sultry spaces," "after hours fun is endless." This is useful framing for what the building's community culture likely looks like.

## 6. Unit Recommendations

Given confirmed structural sound insulation failure, the only units worth considering would be top-floor corner units with no neighbors above and minimal shared wall surface. The building is at 7141-7155 Santa Monica Blvd — a busy stretch of WeHo — so street-facing units will have additional ambient noise from the boulevard. Interior courtyard units may offer some relief from street noise but still share floors/ceilings with neighbors. No floor-by-floor data available. If touring against advice, request specifically a top-floor unit and listen carefully to ambient noise during the visit.

## 7. Open Tour Questions

1. What is the current management company (Lincoln Property Company was listed on Yelp, but the official site now shows SRG Residential in the legal footer) — has there been another management change, and how long has current staff been in place?
2. Are the electrical systems in the specific unit being shown fully functional? Request a walkthrough of every socket and appliance during the tour.
3. What is the actual all-in monthly cost including parking, trash/utility billing, and any mandatory fees beyond base rent?
4. Is there a noise mitigation policy and documented quiet hours enforcement process? How is a noise complaint handled and what is the SLA?
5. What is the current status of the elevators — are both operational, and what is the cleaning/sanitation schedule for common areas?
