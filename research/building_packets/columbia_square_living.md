## 1. Verdict

**TOUR.** Columbia Square Living is one of the strongest candidates in the queue. Greystar management has held up consistently well across multiple independent review platforms spanning 2023 through late 2025, with no management transition or operational collapse in the record. The amenity stack is full — rooftop pool, gym, concierge, EV charging, package receiving, controlled access — and resident sentiment on staff responsiveness and building cleanliness is notably positive. The one confirmed negative is an active nearby construction project (a 276-unit building) that has caused significant daytime noise for window-facing units, which is a material concern for anyone who works from home. That issue needs to be probed directly on tour: which exposures are affected, what the projected timeline is, and whether the building will offer transfers. The fee stack also rises quickly with parking and pet rent added. Neither issue disqualifies the building; both must be verified in person.

## 6. Unit Recommendations

Avoid units facing the active construction project (a 276-unit building reported as going up in front of some windows — specific side not named in review but worth asking leasing which exposures are affected). Upper floors on the El Centro side or interior-facing units are likely to be quieter. Building is described as one of the tallest in Hollywood, so upper floors facing Hollywood Hills or away from Sunset/Hollywood Blvd should have better noise insulation. No specific floor-by-floor data in public reviews; ask leasing directly which exposures face the construction site and what the projected completion date is.

## 7. Open Tour Questions

1. Which unit exposures face the active adjacent construction project, and what is the projected completion date? Is the building offering any rent concessions or transfer options for affected residents?
2. What is the all-in monthly cost for a 1-bed unit including parking, trash, RUBS/utility billing, and any mandatory fees beyond base rent?
3. What is the security deposit structure — the CSV shows a range of $1,500–$10,000, which is unusually wide. What determines where a specific applicant lands in that range?
4. How is the package room operated — staffed desk, locker system, or automated? What is the procedure for after-hours package retrieval?
5. What is the current occupancy rate, and how many units are on month-to-month vs. annual leases? This helps gauge turnover and community stability.
