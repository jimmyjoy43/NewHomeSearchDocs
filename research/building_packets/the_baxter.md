## 1. Verdict

**TOUR — WITH CAUTION.** The Baxter is a brand new 86-unit boutique building that completed construction in 2024. The public review record is essentially empty: zero reviews on ApartmentRatings and Apartments.com, and only 11 Yelp reviews whose text is behind robots.txt. That is an honest data gap, not a failure signal. What is clear from confirmed sources: the building has a good physical package (rooftop, EV charging, package room, on-site parking, in-unit laundry, small-dog-friendly, boutique 86-unit scale), it sits on a quiet side street one block off Hollywood Blvd, and it is new enough that construction defects and management culture have not yet surfaced in the public record. The management company (SD Property Management / livesdproperties.com) is a small local operator with no prominent negative review trail, but also no positive confirmation beyond marketing copy. This should be toured primarily to fill the data gap and assess management quality in person. Two essential amenities are flagged as missing in CSV unit rows (central A/C and controlled access) and must be confirmed on-site. The building's price point ($2,750-$4,150 for 1-2bd) is competitive, parking is confirmed free for residents, and the $1,000 security deposit is reasonable.

## 6. Unit Recommendations

Cherokee Ave is a low-traffic one-way street in Old Hollywood, running between Hollywood Blvd and Franklin. Street-facing units on floors 2-4 should be quieter than comparable Hollywood/Vine or Santa Monica Blvd buildings. Upper floors (5-7) will have better Hollywood Hills views per marketing. Building is 7 stories with 86 units — relatively small footprint. No floor-by-floor noise or exposure data available. Request a unit on floors 5-7 on the Cherokee Ave side for the best view-to-noise ratio. Ask specifically whether units have operable windows or whether the building is mechanically ventilated, since A/C is flagged as missing on some CSV rows.

## 7. Open Tour Questions

1. Is central A/C standard in every unit, or is it only available on select floor plans? The CSV flags it as missing for some units at the 6250 Hollywood Blvd address rows.
2. What does controlled access look like — is there a staffed desk, fob entry only, or video intercom? The CSV flags controlled access as missing on some unit rows.
3. How many of the 86 units are currently occupied, and what is the split between market-rate and the 21 deed-restricted affordable units? How are those units distributed across the building?

5. Are there any pet-specific amenities (dog run, wash station), and what exactly are the pet size/breed restrictions?
