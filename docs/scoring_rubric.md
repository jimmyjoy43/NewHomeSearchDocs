# Scoring Rubric

All scores are 1-5. 1 = unacceptable. 3 = acceptable. 5 = excellent.

## quiet_score
- 5: No noise complaints in reviews. Unit faces interior courtyard or quiet side street.
- 4: Minor noise mentions. No chronic or structural issues.
- 3: Noise mentioned but manageable and not pervasive.
- 2: Noise is a recurring theme across multiple independent reviews.
- 1: Chronic noise complaints, or unit faces a major venue, nightclub, or busy street.

## management_score
- 5: Named staff praised across multiple independent reviews. Fast maintenance response confirmed.
- 4: Generally positive with isolated complaints.
- 3: Mixed. Quality depends on which staff member you get.
- 2: Multiple reviews mention ignored maintenance requests or AI-gated communication.
- 1: Staff rated 1/5 on ApartmentRatings. Confirmed unresponsive. (Reference: Modera Argyle early 2025.)

## amenity_score
- 5: All essential amenities present plus 4 or more nice-to-haves.
- 4: All essentials plus 2-3 nice-to-haves.
- 3: All essentials, no nice-to-haves.
- 2: Missing 1 essential amenity.
- 1: Missing 2 or more essential amenities.

## adult_vibe_score
- 5: Reviews specifically mention quiet community, working professionals, stable long-term tenants.
- 4: No red flags. Community feels settled.
- 3: Mixed. Some party or noise culture present but not dominant.
- 2: Reviews mention rowdy neighbors, loud common areas, or persistent party culture.
- 1: Stunt actors running commercial classes with screaming past 11 PM. (Reference: The Camden, confirmed 2024-2025 resident review.)

## Scoring rules
- Label all evidence as CONFIRMED (from reviews or public record) or INFERRED (from patterns or limited data).
- Do not assign a score of 5 without at least two independent CONFIRMED data points.
- Leave a score blank rather than guess when data is insufficient.
- A management_score of 1 combined with quiet_score of 1 or 2 is an automatic disqualifier. Stop and flag before proceeding.
